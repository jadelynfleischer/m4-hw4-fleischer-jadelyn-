The Chicks

About

The Chicks are an American country band originated from Dallas, Texas. The band consists of Natalie Maines, Martie Maguire, and Emily Strayer. For six years before attracting a major label company or record deal, The Chicks travelled around Texas preforming bluegrass and country music. After signing with Monument Records Nashville in 1997, they achieved major success with their albums Wide Open Spaces and Fly. The Chicks have won 13 Grammy Awards including Album of the Year, Record of the Year, and Song of the Year. By July 2020, with 33 million certified albums sold and $27.9 million of albums sales in the US, the Chicks had become the best-selling all-woman band and best-selling country group in the US.

Controversy

On March 10, 2003, the Dixie Chicks performed at the Shepherd's Bush Empire theater in London, England, and Maines told the audience that the band did not support the imminent Allied invasion of Iraq and were "ashamed" that President George W. Bush was from Texas. After this remark, the Dixie Chicks were blacklisted by thousands of country radio stations and several band members even received death threats. In October 2004, the Dixie Chicks joined the Vote for Change tour, performing in concerts and raised money for political groups opposing Bush. In 2005, Maguire, Strayer, and Maines joined with 31 other recording artists supporting relationships of all kinds, regardless of sexual orientation or gender identity, on a two-disc release titled Love Rocks, with their song from the album Home called "I Believe in Love".
